About the Project

The ELECT Project is an international initiative led by Mulungushi University, Zambia, in partnership with Copperbelt University, Aalto University (Finland), and Hellenic Open University (Greece).

The project focuses on developing and promoting energy-efficient lighting solutions to support Zambia's transition to clean energy, while building local capacity through education and innovation at Mulungushi University.

Target Beneficiaries:
  • University students & researchers
  • Local communities & households
  • Energy sector professionals
  • Policy makers & government

Objectives & Expected Impacts

Main Objectives
  • Develop innovative lighting technologies
  • Enhance clean energy curriculum
  • Build research capacity
  • Promote adoption in communities
Expected Outcomes
  • 30-40% energy reduction
  • 500+ trained professionals
  • 10+ lighting prototypes
  • Enhanced university curricula
Long-term Benefits
  • Contribution to Zambia's energy goals
  • Reduced carbon emissions
  • Lower electricity costs
  • Sustainable knowledge transfer
